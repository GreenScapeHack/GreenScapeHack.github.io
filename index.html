Current Challanges

In Waste Segregation

Lack of Standardization

Different regions and municipalities have varying rules and regulations for waste segregation, leading to confusion and inconsistency.

Human Error

Even with clear guidelines, human error can still occur in waste segregation, leading to contamination and improper disposal.

Limited Resource

Municipalities and waste management companies may have limited resources for waste segregation, making it difficult to efficiently and effectively sort and dispose of waste.

Contamination

Contamination occurs when non-recyclable items are mixed with recyclables. This makes it challenging to process and recycle materials effectively.

Landfill Expensive

Overfilling landfills is a common issue, causing environmental problems and limited landfill capacity for the future.

Expensive

Waste segregation is expensive due to the need for specialized equipment, labor, and infrastructure for sorting, disposal, and managing different waste streams, increasing overall operationalÂ costs.
